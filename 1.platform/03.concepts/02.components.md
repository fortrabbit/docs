## Intro

With VPS hosting you pay for size of your box. Within that box you can run different services. fortrabbit on the other side has a micro service oriented architecture with services running in isolated containers. This architecture is reflected in the pricing. A component represents a service that can be booked and scaled individually per [environment](/1.platform/10.objects/02.environment.md).

PHP, database, but also traffic and storage are components. Most components are available in different sizes, those are called plans and are titled like t-shirt labels: XS, S, M, L â€¦. Different resource attributes are associated with the plans as well.

| **Component** | **Attribute** | **Plan** | **Price** |
| ------------- | ------------- | -------: | --------: |
| PHP           | XXX MB        |       XS |        $X |
| Database      | XXX MB        |       MD |        $X |

See the [components list](/1.platform/09.components/index.md) for all available components. The [pricing page](https://www.fortrabbit.com/pricing) gives you an overview. The [pricing intro](/1.platform/12.billing/01.pricing-structure.md) explains level billing concepts.

## Required and optional components

The components PHP, traffic and storage are required, they can not be deselected. All other components are optional.

## Components during the free trial

All components are available with the [free trial](/1.platform/02.get-started/02.free-trial.md). They are however limited to the smallest plan.
